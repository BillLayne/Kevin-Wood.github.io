Knowledge is Power

What Your Coverage Means For You

Understanding your auto insurance is key. Here’s a simple guide to what each part of your policy does to protect you on the road.

Bodily Injury & Property Damage Liability

This is the core of your policy. If you're at fault in an accident, it pays for the other party's medical bills (Bodily Injury) and vehicle or property repairs (Property Damage).

Your limits ($50k/$100k/$50k) are significantly higher than the NC state minimums ($30k/$60k/$25k), providing you with better financial protection.

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist

Protects you if you're hit by a driver who has no insurance (Uninsured) or not enough insurance (Underinsured) to cover your medical expenses. This is crucial coverage.

Medical Payments

Covers medical and funeral expenses for you or your passengers after an accident, regardless of who is at fault. It provides quick access to funds for initial treatment.

Other Than Collision (Comprehensive)

Pays for damage to your car from non-collision events like theft, vandalism, fire, hail, or hitting an animal. You selected a $100 deductible, which means you pay the first $100 of the repair cost.
