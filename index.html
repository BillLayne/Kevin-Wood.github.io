Understanding Your Auto Insurance

Bodily Injury Liability

Covers medical expenses for others injured in an accident you cause. North Carolina requires minimum $30,000/$60,000 coverage (increasing to $50,000/$100,000 on July 1, 2025).

Property Damage Liability

Pays for damage to others' property in an accident you cause. Current NC minimum is $25,000 (increasing to $50,000 on July 1, 2025).

Uninsured Motorist Coverage

Protects you if you're hit by a driver without insurance. Your policy includes $50,000/$100,000 UM/UIM coverage plus $50,000 property damage.

Comprehensive Coverage

Protects against theft, vandalism, weather damage, and other non-collision incidents. Your deductible of $100 keeps costs low while providing excellent protection.
